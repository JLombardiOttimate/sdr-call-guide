# **ERP Landscape Analysis: Hospitality Prospects**

**Analysis Date:** March 26, 2026 **Dataset:** 204 hospitality deals (96 closed-won, 108 closed-lost) | ~878 call transcripts **Territory:** Hospitality sector (Country Clubs, Hotels/Resorts, Restaurants, Golf Clubs, Casinos, Specialty)

---

## **Executive Summary**

The hospitality mid-market runs on club management systems. Jonas Club Software, NorthStar Club Management, and ClubEssential together appear in approximately 82 of 204 deals, making the club management ERP ecosystem the dominant accounting infrastructure across the largest sub-vertical Ottimate sells into. Beyond club systems, the ERP landscape fragments quickly — QuickBooks shows up in smaller single-entity operations, Sage Intacct appears in mid-market multi-entity hospitality management companies, M3/Infor serves the hotel segment, Restaurant 365 appears in multi-unit restaurant chains, and Microsoft Dynamics (Great Plains / Business Central) appears across several segments with mixed results.

The most important finding isn't which ERP prospects use — it's how ERP status determines deal outcomes. Club management ERPs (Jonas, NorthStar, ClubEssential) convert at 57–78%, while certain ERPs create structural disadvantages — Great Plains deals convert at only 20%, Acumatica at 0%, and Sage 100/300 (legacy) at 0% in this dataset. An estimated 24 deals were lost purely because the prospect was mid-ERP-migration. Understanding which ERP a prospect runs should be a first-call qualification question.

This report covers ERPs (systems of record) separately from AP automation and payment tools (which Ottimate competes with or displaces). These are fundamentally different categories — Ottimate integrates with ERPs but replaces AP tools.

**Methodology note:** Primary ERP classification is based on context-aware reading of all 204 deal analysis files and sampling of 60+ call transcripts. Where the deal analysis mentioned an ERP as the prospect's operating system, that was classified as primary. Where the ERP was only listed as a compatible integration by the Ottimate rep, it was excluded. Some deals mention multiple systems (e.g., Jonas for club management + Sage Intacct for GL); in those cases, the GL/accounting system was treated as the primary ERP. Approximately 15–20 deals had no clearly identified ERP in available documentation.

### **Jonas Club Software — The Beachhead**

**Prevalence:** 46 of 204 deals (23%) — by far the most common ERP in the pipeline.

**Where it shows up:** Country clubs (Carmel, Denver, Boca Woods, Admiral's Cove, Raintree, Indian Wells), private clubs (Metropolitan Club SF, Missouri Athletic Club), and yacht clubs (Avalon, Bay Head). Jonas is the default mid-market club management platform — it handles member billing, POS, tee times, dining reservations, and general ledger accounting in a single system.

**Win dynamics:** Ottimate's Jonas integration is mature and was a positive factor in virtually every closed-won club deal. At Carmel Country Club, Stephanie Anderson (CFO) ran Jonas for POS and member billing but was using Beanworks for AP — Ottimate replaced Beanworks and connected natively to Jonas. At Denver Country Club, Courtney Hunt (Controller) used Jonas alongside her existing workflows, and Ottimate bridged both. The integration was never a friction point in closed-won Jonas deals — it was table stakes.

**Loss dynamics:** Thirteen Jonas deals closed-lost, but the losses were predominantly driven by absent economic buyers and timing issues, not ERP incompatibility. Metropolitan Club SF's Controller loved the product — *"I did share this with my staff and they seem to like it"* — but a conservative new board and recent mismanagement recovery killed the deal. Bay Head Yacht Club experienced check fraud but chose a banking solution. Country Club of Darien stayed with Eden Red due to an existing contract.

**The risk within the risk:** Jonas has its own native invoice entry capability within its GL module. For smaller clubs with low invoice volumes (under 100/month), some Controllers view Jonas's built-in entry as "good enough" and don't see the ROI of adding another system. This is the status quo objection — not a competitive ERP risk, but a "why add complexity" question.

**Qualification guidance:** If a prospect says "Jonas" and has 3+ departments coding invoices, this is your highest-priority lead. Jonas deals convert at 72% — the only question is whether the economic buyer is engaged. Confirm on Call 1: "Is the Controller/CFO available for the next conversation?" If yes, proceed aggressively.

### **NorthStar Club Management — The Highest Converter**

**Prevalence:** 18 deals (14 closed-won, 4 closed-lost). **78% win rate.**

**Why it converts at 78%:** NorthStar is a direct Jonas competitor in the club management space, but it has weaker native AP capabilities than Jonas. Organizations running NorthStar feel the AP gap more acutely — there's no built-in approval workflow, no automated GL coding, and the invoice entry process is manual. Ottimate fills a clear capability gap that NorthStar can't address natively.

At Orchid Island Golf & Beach Club, Jackie Nickel (Controller) described managing 20+ vendors on prepaid modules within NorthStar — a manual tracking nightmare. At Sarasota National, the GM looked at the pricing and said *"Looks cost neutral to me"* — the NorthStar pain was clear enough that ROI was obvious. At Cypress Point, the prospect ran NorthStar and Katie demonstrated PDF URL pushes directly into NorthStar's AP header.

**Segment context:** NorthStar shops are typically mid-sized country clubs (200–600 members) that chose NorthStar over Jonas for specific club management features (golf ops, dining). They tend to have 1–2 finance staff who handle everything — AP is a disproportionate time drain relative to their strategic role.

**Qualification guidance:** NorthStar + country club + 2+ entities or departments = your highest-priority lead in the entire pipeline. Seventy-eight percent of these close. Move fast.

### **ClubEssential — The Third Club Pillar**

**Prevalence:** 18 deals (10 closed-won, 8 closed-lost). **56% win rate.**

**Where it shows up:** Country clubs (Edina, Quail Hollow, Biltmore, Olympia Fields, Springs Club, La Quinta, DC Ranch), yacht clubs (Centerport), and golf clubs (Woodbridge, Territory). ClubEssential is the third major club management ERP alongside Jonas and NorthStar, particularly strong in larger metropolitan area clubs.

**Win dynamics:** At Edina Country Club, the prospect had recently completed their ClubEssential implementation and was ready to layer AP automation on top. Morgan Macko noted that the ClubEssential integration was smooth, and the deal progressed through standard demo-to-pricing flow. At Quail Hollow Club, Margaret Bidwell (Director of Finance) ran ClubEssential and the deal closed through a 6-call process. Multiple Katie Snowberger wins (Biltmore, Olympia Fields, Springs Club, DC Ranch) were ClubEssential clubs where the integration was a known quantity.

**Loss dynamics:** Eight ClubEssential deals closed-lost. Dorset Field Club, Midland Country Club, Riviera Country Club, San Luis Obispo Country Club, Sonnenalp Club, Tampa Yacht & Country Club, Territory Golf & Country Club, and Bonita Bay Community Association all lost — primarily due to the same patterns seen elsewhere: absent economic buyers, budget timing issues, and competing priorities. The losses were not driven by ClubEssential integration concerns.

**Why it converts lower than Jonas/NorthStar (56% vs. 72–78%):** ClubEssential has somewhat more capable native AP workflows than NorthStar, reducing the urgency for external AP automation. Some ClubEssential clubs feel their current system handles invoice entry adequately, particularly those with lower volumes. The pain threshold for adding Ottimate is slightly higher.

**Qualification guidance:** ClubEssential deals follow the same club playbook as Jonas and NorthStar — qualify on department count, invoice volume, and economic buyer access. The 56% win rate is solid but requires the prospect to feel genuine AP pain beyond what ClubEssential provides natively. Ask: "How are invoices getting coded and approved today?" If the answer involves manual processes and paper routing, proceed. If they say "ClubEssential handles most of it," dig deeper on what's still manual.

### **QuickBooks — Small but Fast**

**Prevalence:** 27 deals (13 closed-won, 14 closed-lost). **48% win rate.**

**Win dynamics:** QuickBooks deals close quickly (average 38 days). Equator Coffees ran QBO and hit a ceiling at 400–500 invoices per week across 12 locations — QBO's approval add-on had already failed them, validating the need for external AP automation before the first demo. Noi Due Carne's owner described spending 2 hours per day on scanning alone. The QB pain is real: no approval workflows, no automated coding, no audit trail, no multi-entity consolidation.

**Loss dynamics:** Fourteen QuickBooks deals closed-lost, often because the organization was too small to justify the investment, or the decision-maker couldn't be reached. Ada Street (single restaurant in Chicago) was a one-call deal that went nowhere. Los Angeles Ale Works (brewery) had a single call with no follow-up. QB shops with under 100 invoices/month and a single entity may not have enough volume to justify the cost.

**Segment context:** QBO organizations tend to be simpler deals with shorter cycles and lower ACV. The pain is real but the deal size is smaller. These are good quick wins for pipeline velocity but not the foundation of a hospitality GTM strategy.

**Qualification guidance:** Qualify on invoice volume and multi-location complexity. Single-entity QBO shops with under 100 invoices/month may not have enough pain to justify the investment. Multi-location QBO shops (Equator Coffees profile — 10+ locations, 300+ invoices/week, growing) are strong targets.

### **Sage Intacct — The Multi-Entity Play**

**Prevalence:** 14 deals (8 closed-won, 6 closed-lost). **57% win rate.**

**Where it shows up:** Unlike healthcare where Sage Intacct dominates, hospitality sees it primarily in multi-entity hospitality management companies, hotel groups, and larger operations that have outgrown QuickBooks. Sree Hotels, Discovery Land, Stepstone Corporate, Wedgewood Weddings, Chicken Ranch Casino, and several others run Sage Intacct as their GL system of record.

**Win dynamics:** Ottimate's Sage Intacct integration — the same one that dominates in healthcare — is a proven differentiator. At Sree Hotels, Mitan Patel (Director of Accounting) needed multi-property GL mapping that pushed directly into Sage Intacct, and Ottimate delivered. At Discovery Land, a complex multi-club operation, the Intacct integration handled multi-dimensional coding (department, location, class) seamlessly.

**Loss dynamics:** Six Sage Intacct deals closed-lost. Silo Ridge Field Club ran Sage Intacct but lost on multi-entity complexity concerns. NCG Hotels and RLM Investments were lost to timing and competitive dynamics, not integration issues. Pita Jungle ran Intacct with a 3-entity structure but lost due to decision-maker absence.

**Qualification guidance:** Sage Intacct in hospitality signals a more sophisticated finance operation. These prospects have already made a deliberate ERP choice and are likely evaluating AP automation with clear criteria. Proceed as you would in healthcare — the integration is proven. Confirm they are live on Intacct (not mid-migration) and that the economic buyer is accessible.

### **M3 / Infor — The Hotel Accounting System**

**Prevalence:** 14 deals (7 closed-won, 7 closed-lost). **50% win rate.**

**Where it shows up:** Hotel management companies, multi-property hotel operators, and resort groups. Radiate Hospitality, Vivamee Hospitality, Stepstone Corporate, Westport Hospitality, Coco Key Resort, and Main & Vine all run M3 or other Infor products on the won side. Desai Hotel Group, Ivy Hospitality, Stay Hospitality, Magnolia Hospitality Services, Ponderosa Hotel Management, and Reneson Hotels are on the lost side.

**Win dynamics:** At Radiate Hospitality, Ferdinand Widjaja (Regional Director, Finance) championed the deal through 6 calls — he understood the M3 integration requirements and Ottimate delivered. At Coco Key Resort, the M3 accounting integration was demonstrated successfully. The 50% win rate shows M3 deals are winnable but require more effort than club deals.

**Loss dynamics:** Seven M3/Infor deals closed-lost. The losses share a common pattern: complex multi-property operations with 5+ stakeholders, long evaluation cycles, and decision-making that involves both the management company and individual property GMs. Desai Hotel Group and Ivy Hospitality both stalled due to multi-threaded decision complexity. Magnolia Hospitality had a single 22-minute call before ghosting.

**Why it's harder:** Hotel management companies on M3 have a fundamentally different buying process than country clubs. The accounting function is centralized at the management company level, but property-level AP staff do the daily work. The decision to add AP automation requires buy-in at both levels — corporate finance and property operations.

**Qualification guidance:** M3/Infor deals are viable (50% win rate) but require a multi-property sales motion: engage corporate finance AND at least one property-level champion. Ask on Call 1: "How many properties? Who at the management company needs to approve? Can we get the corporate Controller on the next call?" If engagement at the corporate level is confirmed, these deals are worth pursuing.

### **Restaurant 365 — The Restaurant ERP**

**Prevalence:** 7 deals (3 closed-won, 4 closed-lost). **43% win rate.**

**Where it shows up:** Multi-unit restaurant chains and food service operations. Barcade, Cherry Hills Country Club, and Hillsboro Club on the won side. HCI Hospitality, Lyons Group, MasMex, and Primanti Bros on the lost side.

**Win dynamics:** Restaurant 365 is increasingly common as the default accounting/operations platform for multi-unit restaurants. When it appears in a club setting (Cherry Hills, Hillsboro), it's usually handling food & beverage operations alongside a separate club management ERP. Ottimate's value is in automating the AP workflow that R365 doesn't natively handle well — particularly multi-vendor invoice capture and approval routing.

**Loss dynamics:** HCI Hospitality needed bakery vendor EDI feeds (Bimbo, Flowers Bakery — 3–5 weekly invoices × 40 locations) that Ottimate couldn't automate as well as the R365 pipeline. Primanti Bros stalled across multiple calls with no clear champion. MasMex deferred to *"probably August"* due to an accounting firm transition.

**Qualification guidance:** R365 deals are viable but require understanding what R365 handles natively (food costing, recipe management, some invoice capture) vs. where it falls short (multi-entity AP workflows, approval routing, virtual card payments). If the prospect's pain is specifically about invoice approval and payment automation beyond what R365 offers, proceed.

### **NetSuite — Evaluate Carefully**

**Prevalence:** 7 deals (3 closed-won, 4 closed-lost). **43% win rate.**

**Where it shows up:** PE-backed restaurant groups, hotel management companies, and multi-entity hospitality operations. NetSuite appears when the organization has outgrown QuickBooks and made a deliberate enterprise ERP decision.

**Win dynamics:** Three deals closed successfully despite NetSuite's native AP module. These were typically organizations with complex multi-entity structures where NetSuite's built-in AP was insufficient — Leisure Hotels & Resorts, Irving Farm Coffee Roasters, and Everyday Coffee Company all had specific needs (multi-location GL mapping, approval workflows, virtual card payments) that NetSuite's native module couldn't satisfy.

**Loss dynamics:** SeoulSpice ran NetSuite and the native AP module was deemed sufficient at their low invoice volume. Pala Casino, Polly Management, and TEAM Schostak Family Restaurants also lost. When invoice volume is low and entity structure is simple, NetSuite's bundled AP is "good enough."

**Qualification guidance:** Ask on Call 1: "How many entities and locations do you have? What's your monthly invoice volume?" If they have 3+ entities and 200+ invoices/month, Ottimate can demonstrate clear value beyond NetSuite's native module. If they're single-entity with under 150 invoices/month, NetSuite's built-in AP likely meets their needs. Also ask: "Was your NetSuite purchase PE-driven?" — if yes, the AP decision may have already been made at the fund level.

### **Great Plains — Legacy Risk**

**Prevalence:** 5 deals (1 closed-won, 4 closed-lost). **20% win rate.**

**What happens:** Great Plains is a legacy Microsoft ERP that's being sunset in favor of Dynamics 365 Business Central. Prospects on Great Plains are often either (a) considering migration to Business Central/Intacct/NetSuite, which stalls the AP automation decision, or (b) have entrenched workflows built around Great Plains that make adding new tools complex.

Caribe Royale (lost) chose Wells Fargo native AP instead. Elite Casino Resorts (lost) had PO matching complexity and cloud skepticism from upper management. See Rock City (lost) had existing AP tool retention. Morton Golf Management (lost) ghosted after strong May calls.

**Qualification guidance:** Great Plains signals an organization that may be mid-migration planning. Ask: "Are you considering upgrading from Great Plains?" If yes, this deal may need to be parked until the migration decision is made. If they're staying on Great Plains long-term, qualify on whether Ottimate's GP integration meets their workflow needs.

---

### **Sage 100/300 (Legacy) — Structural Disadvantage**

**Prevalence:** 3 deals, all closed-lost. **0% win rate.**

**What happens:** Sage 100 and Sage 300 are legacy on-premise Sage products. Blue Lake Casino, Recreation Centers of Sun City Arizona, and Reneson Hotels all ran these systems and all lost. Like Great Plains, these are aging ERPs where the prospect may be more focused on ERP modernization than AP automation.

**Qualification guidance:** Sage 100/300 signals a legacy environment. These prospects may need to migrate to Sage Intacct before AP automation makes sense. If they're planning to stay on Sage 100/300 long-term, qualify on whether Ottimate's integration supports their version.

## **Part 2: AP Automation & Payment Tools (Competitors / Incumbents)**

These are the tools that sit on top of the ERP and handle invoice capture, approval workflows, payment execution, or some combination. Ottimate doesn't integrate with these — it replaces them.

### **Competitive Landscape Table**

| Tool | Category | Deals Encountered | Won Against | Lost To | Competitive Dynamic |
| ----- | ----- | ----- | ----- | ----- | ----- |
| Bill.com | AP Automation + Payments | 26+ | 20+ | 3 | Prospects outgrow it at scale; Ottimate wins on GL depth, multi-entity support, and payment reliability. Equator Coffees: Bill.com held large payments for a week. |
| Beanworks | AP Automation | 19 | 14+ | 2 | Carmel CC CFO: *"flip back and forth between Beanworks...is a big fat waste of time."* Lacks payment automation. Easy displacement except when under contract. |
| Eden Red | Virtual Card + Rebates | 14 | 7+ | 4 | Strongest named competitor in hospitality. Boca Woods CFO: *"We never came anywhere near what they estimated."* Wins on existing relationships at clubs. |
| CSI / Corpay | Virtual Card Payments | 13 | 7+ | 0 | Payment-only tool. Denver CC used CSI but couldn't handle two-signature checks over $10K. Displaced by bundling AP + payments. |
| AvidXchange | AP Automation + Payments | 5 | 3+ | 1 | Perceived as enterprise/expensive. Mid-market hospitality prospects view it as overkill for their needs. |
| Coupa | Unified Procurement + AP | 2 | 0 | 2 | "All-in-one platform" beats point solution story at larger organizations. Not common in hospitality mid-market. |
| Stampli | AP Automation | 2 | 1 | 1 | Pine Needles lost to Stampli. Allocation capability is Ottimate's differentiator when competing head-to-head. |
| Punch | Restaurant AP | 1 | 0 | 1 | Won Partake Collective — niche restaurant competitor. Only threatens restaurant segment. |

### **Key Competitive Patterns**

**Ottimate wins consistently when displacing Bill.com, Beanworks, CSI, or Eden Red.** These tools have clear capability gaps that prospects already feel — Bill.com can't handle multi-location GL mapping at scale, Beanworks lacks payment execution, CSI is payment-only without AP automation, and Eden Red over-promises on virtual card rebates. When a prospect is on one of these tools and hits a ceiling, Ottimate is the natural upgrade path.

**The Eden Red exception:** Eden Red is the most competitive named threat in hospitality. It won 4 deals outright (Country Club of Darien, Rancho La Quinta, NCG Hotels, See Rock City) and retained existing business at several others. Eden Red's strength is its virtual card rebate program and existing relationships with club and hotel operators. The counter: Boca Woods' CFO Rick Cooke was explicit about Eden Red's failure to deliver — *"We went through that with Eden Red. And that was the big selling feature for them. We never came anywhere near what they estimated."* This is a powerful competitive proof point.

**Ottimate loses to bundled platforms (Coupa) and contract lock-in (Beanworks, Eden Red).** Coupa won at larger organizations where the CEO wanted fewer vendors. Beanworks and Eden Red retained deals where existing contracts created switching costs. The competitive counter: Ottimate's advantage is hospitality specialization (Jonas/NorthStar/ClubEssential integration, club-specific GL mapping, department-level approval workflows), faster time-to-value, and lower total cost.

## **Part 4: ERP-Driven Qualification Framework**

### **Green Light — High Conversion (Proceed Aggressively)**

**NorthStar + Country Club:** 78% win rate. Limited native AP automation, acute department-level coding pain, and prospects self-select for Ottimate's value prop. If they say "NorthStar" and have 3+ departments, this is the highest-priority lead in your pipeline.

**Jonas + Multi-Department Club:** 72% win rate. The most common ERP in your pipeline and your integration is mature. Jonas clubs with 200+ invoices/month and multiple department heads doing approval/coding are your sweet spot. TrueClub partnership pricing accelerates these further.

**Sage Intacct (Any Segment):** 57% win rate. Proven integration from healthcare carries over to hospitality. These are typically more sophisticated buyers with clear criteria.

**ClubEssential + Active Pain:** 56% win rate. Solid conversion when the prospect articulates specific AP pain beyond what ClubEssential handles natively.

### **Yellow Light — Qualify Carefully**

**M3/Infor (Hotel Management):** 50% win rate — viable but requires multi-property, multi-stakeholder engagement. Qualify on corporate-level decision authority.

**QuickBooks (Multi-Location):** 48% win rate overall, higher for multi-location operations with 300+ invoices/month. Confirm invoice volume justifies investment.

**NetSuite (High Complexity):** 43% win rate. Only pursue if 3+ entities and 200+ invoices/month. Single-entity NetSuite shops likely won't convert.

**Restaurant 365:** 43% win rate. Qualify on what R365 handles natively vs. where the gaps are.

### **Red Light — Likely Loss (Deprioritize)**

**Great Plains (Any Status):** 20% win rate. Legacy ERP, often mid-migration planning. Unless the prospect is committed to staying on GP long-term with a clear AP pain, park the deal.

**Sage 100/300 (Legacy):** 0% win rate. Three deals, all lost. These prospects may need ERP modernization before AP automation.

**Acumatica (PO Matching Required):** 0% win rate. Product gap on PO ingestion. Until resolved, high-risk.

**Any ERP Mid-Migration:** An estimated 24 of 108 lost deals involved prospects mid-ERP-migration. If the prospect says "we're implementing [new system]," establish a re-engagement date 60 days post-go-live rather than investing further cycles now.

## **Part 5: Strategic Implications**

### **The Pipeline Math**

If you apply ERP-based qualification to your current dataset:

* **An estimated 30+ of 108 lost deals (~28%)** were on ERPs with low win rates (Great Plains, Sage 100/300, Acumatica) or were mid-ERP-migration. These deals consumed sales cycles but had near-zero probability of closing.
* Removing them from the denominator, your **adjusted win rate improves meaningfully from 47% to approximately 55%**.
* The issue isn't pipeline generation — it's pipeline quality. You're building pipeline on ERPs where conversion is structurally challenging.

### **The Club Management ERP Opportunity**

Jonas, NorthStar, and ClubEssential combined represent 82 deals (40% of total volume) and contain your highest-converting segments. A club-management-ERP-first GTM strategy — co-marketing with Jonas, presence at Jonas user conferences, NorthStar and ClubEssential partnerships, TrueClub expansion, and club-specific case studies — would concentrate resources on the ERP ecosystem where you have the strongest product-market fit. These three ERPs alone produced 57 of your 96 wins (59%).

### **The Sage Intacct Bridge**

Sage Intacct converts at 57% in hospitality and even higher in healthcare. The integration is proven across both verticals. For hospitality management companies that have outgrown QuickBooks and adopted Sage Intacct, Ottimate is the natural AP automation layer. Leverage the healthcare Sage Intacct case studies and integration depth as a cross-vertical asset.

### **The M3/Hotel Opportunity**

M3/Infor deals are a 50/50 bet today — better than expected, but requiring significantly more sales effort than club deals. Seven won deals prove the market is winnable. The structural challenge is the multi-stakeholder buying process at hotel management companies. A targeted hotel management play — corporate-level engagement, property-level champion development, M3-specific case studies from Radiate Hospitality, Vivamee, and Stepstone — could tilt this toward the club-level conversion rates.

### **The Restaurant 365 Watch**

Restaurant 365 is growing rapidly as the default restaurant operations platform. Seven deals in your pipeline is meaningful, and the 43% win rate shows it's winnable. As R365 adoption grows in the multi-unit restaurant segment, Ottimate needs a clear positioning story: R365 handles food costing and operations; Ottimate handles AP automation, approval workflows, and vendor payments. Building an R365 integration case study now creates a defensible position in a growing segment.
