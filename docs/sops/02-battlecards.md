# SOP: Updating Battlecards

## What is a battlecard?

A battlecard is a competitive reference card that appears in the "Battlecards" tab of the SDR Call Guide. Each card covers one competitor and gives SDRs the positioning, talk tracks, and trap questions they need when a prospect is evaluating that vendor.

## When to use this SOP

- You want to add a battlecard for a new competitor
- You want to update an existing battlecard (new positioning, updated strengths/weaknesses, new trap questions)
- You've learned something new about a competitor from a deal and want it captured

## What you'll need to provide

A complete battlecard has 8 sections. All 8 are required for a new card.

### 1. Name

The competitor's name as SDRs would know it.

**Example:** "Stampli"

### 2. Tagline

One-line competitive positioning. Honest, specific, and memorable.

**Example:** "Good at collaboration, weak on vertical depth."

### 3. Overview

2-3 sentences on who they are, what they do, and who they target.

**Example:** "Stampli is a communication-centric AP platform that focuses on approver collaboration. They sell horizontally across industries and position around workflow speed rather than vertical-specific features."

### 4. When you see them (2-3 signals)

Situations or things a prospect might say that indicate they're looking at this vendor.

**Example:**
- "Prospect mentions approval workflows or approver collaboration as a priority"
- "Prospect is in a general vertical, not a specific one like hospitality or grocery"

### 5. Where we win (3-5 advantages)

Each advantage has a short headline and a 1-2 sentence explanation.

**Example:**
- **Vertical depth:** "Ottimate has purpose-built workflows for hospitality (USALI, multi-property GL), grocery (Item Validation, DSD), and healthcare (PHI redaction, HIPAA). Stampli has none of these."
- **AI-first capture:** "No templates or stencils. Ottimate's AI is trained on 10+ years of invoice data and handles complex, handwritten, multi-page invoices out of the box."

### 6. Where they win (2-3 strengths)

Honest acknowledgment of where the competitor is strong. SDRs need the truth so they're not caught off guard.

**Example:**
- **Collaboration UX:** "Stampli has a clean approval interface with conversation threads on every invoice. Their collaboration workflow is genuinely good."

### 7. Trap questions (2-3 questions)

Discovery questions designed to expose the competitor's weaknesses without being aggressive.

**Example:**
- "Can you show me how your system handles USALI-compliant GL mapping across 15 properties with different chart structures?"

### 8. Landmine

One high-impact question or insight to plant early in the conversation. This is the one thing that makes the prospect question the competitor.

**Example:** "Ask any vendor to demo multi-entity invoice routing with split GL coding across three cost centers. If they can't do it live, they can't do it at all."

## How to prepare a new battlecard using an AI tool

Copy the prompt below into Claude, Gemini, or ChatGPT. Replace `[COMPETITOR]` with the vendor name before running it.

---

**Prompt to copy:**

```
Generate a competitive battlecard for Ottimate vs. [COMPETITOR] in the AP automation space.

About Ottimate:
Ottimate is an AI-powered AP automation platform purpose-built for three verticals:
hospitality, grocery, and healthcare. Key differentiators:

- AI-first invoice capture: No templates or stencils. AI trained on 10+ years of invoice
  data. Handles complex, multi-page, handwritten, and non-standard invoices.
- Line-item validation: Captures and validates at the line-item level, not just headers.
  Powers Item Validation in grocery and PO matching in hospitality.
- Vertical-specific workflows: USALI compliance and multi-property GL mapping for
  hospitality. DSD handling, cost file matching, and Item Validation for grocery.
  PHI redaction and HIPAA compliance for healthcare.
- Multi-entity support: Built for operators running 10-100+ locations with different GL
  structures, approval chains, and cost centers per entity.
- Cost recovery: Duplicate detection, vendor overcharge flagging, and price variance
  alerts that directly recover dollars.
- VendorPay: Check, ACH, and virtual card payments with a rebate model.

Write the battlecard with these exact sections:

1. NAME - Competitor name
2. TAGLINE - One-line competitive positioning (keep it honest and specific)
3. OVERVIEW - 2-3 sentences: what they do, who they target, how they position
4. WHEN YOU SEE THEM - 2-3 signals that a prospect is evaluating this vendor
5. WHERE WE WIN - 3-5 advantages. For each, provide:
   - Area (short headline)
   - Detail (1-2 sentences explaining the advantage)
6. WHERE THEY WIN - 2-3 honest competitor strengths. For each:
   - Area (short headline)
   - Detail (1-2 sentences acknowledging the strength)
7. TRAP QUESTIONS - 2-3 discovery questions that expose gaps in the competitor
8. LANDMINE - One high-impact question or insight to plant early in the conversation

Constraints:
- No em dashes. Use commas, periods, or rewrite.
- Be honest. SDRs need the truth, including where the competitor is strong.
- Be specific. Reference actual features, not generic claims.
- Write for an SDR who has 30 seconds to scan this before a call.
```
