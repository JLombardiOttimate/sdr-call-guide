# **How to Use This Playbook**

Scripts are organized by WHO you're talking to (persona), then WHY they might buy (trigger). Every script has vertical-specific language you can swap in for Hospitality, Grocery, or Healthcare. Every hook includes a quantified value — never pitch without a number.

### **The Five Hook Frames**

* "Do you ever...?" — Surface emotional pain through recognition ("Do you ever find yourself working weekends?")  
* "Imagine if..." — Paint a future state that solves the pain  
* "Your competitor..." — Use competitive or peer comparisons  
* "Based on my research..." — Data-driven personalization tied to their business  
* Customer Stories — Proof points from operators like them

### **Key Principles**

* Lead with operational pain (Controllers) or financial impact (CFOs)  
* If you don't know the trigger before the call, start with the Generic Opener to surface one  
* Move proof points INTO the Pitch section as 'Recap \+ Proof'  
* Include vertical-specific stats where they exist  
* Defer to AE if ERP-specific questions come up

# **SECTION 7: ROLE-PLAY SCENARIOS**

## **Scenario 1: The Skeptical Controller**

**Setup**  
*You're calling a mid-market restaurant group. The Controller answers. She's skeptical of new tools and says her team is already "pretty efficient." Your job: unearth why they're thinking about change (or create the why).*

### **Personality**

Controllers are process-focused and risk-averse. They need proof before they move. Don't pitch — ask. When they say "we're efficient," they mean "I haven't been convinced otherwise."

### **Coaching Notes**

* Open with a Frame 1 question: "Do you ever find yourself working weekends?"  
* When she says no, pivot to discovery: "Walk me through month-end close."  
* Listen for the word "we" — that's your entry. (We code, we approve, we close.)  
* Mirror her language. If she says "we're careful," say "I like that you're careful. How do you make sure nothing slips through?"  
* When you hear a pain point, name it: "It sounds like duplicate invoices are a blind spot." Let her confirm or deny.  
* Close for a meeting: "Would it make sense to spend 20 minutes showing you how other \[verticals\] handle this?"

## **Scenario 2: The Cost-Focused CFO**

**Setup**
